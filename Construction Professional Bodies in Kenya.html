PROFESSIONAL BODIES IN KENYA

Building generally raises several questions, especially for clients with no prior construction knowledge. As a client and employer, the biggest question is who to hire of the very many professionals involved. Knowing where to look or where to ask goes a long way in easing you into the process. In your quest to hire a professional, you realize there are several roles to consider, architects, quantity surveyors, and contractors. Unlike the first two, the contractor umbrella encompasses a plethora of professionals, all as important.

As a professional especially when still green in the industry it is important to know what circles and associations to be a part of, relative to your specific profession. These help us network and push our careers, this is through mentorship programs, expert support and specialised guidance. There are several professional bodies in Kenya tailored for the various professions.

Board of Registered Architects and Quantity Surveyors (BORAQS)

BORAQS, created in 1934, is a regulatory board that registers Architects and Quantity Surveyors, it also maintains professional ethics and industry standards. They also provide training and ensure compliance with government regulations.

Institute of Quantity Surveyors of Kenya (IQSK)

IQSK, operational since 1994, is an organisation that promotes and safeguards the interests of Kenyan Quantity Surveyors. Its primary focus is advancing the profession. They provide membership programs for students and practising professionals, qualifications to register as qualified Quantity Surveyors, journals with the latest news and trends in the industry and events & activities for its members.

Architectural Association of Kenya (AAK)

AAK, established in 1967, is an association for professionals in the built and natural environment, Quantity Surveyors, Architects, planners, Engineers, Interior designers, you name them. It's structured to include membership programs and activities for its members. Membership tiers include students, technicians, graduates and registered professionals. AAK aims to promote excellence and uplift its members while creating a bridge between the professionals and stakeholders in the construction industry, from developers, financial institutions, manufacturers and policymakers.

Engineers Board of Kenya (EBK)

EBK, formerly Engineers Registration Board (ERB) was established in 2010 by an act of parliament. Its primary objective is to develop and regulate the engineering practice in Kenya. Engineers seeking to practice in the country must register with EBK which approves qualified applicants. EBK also has a membership program that supports and provides career growth opportunities for its members.

Association of Construction Managers of Kenya (ACMK)

ACMK is a professional body that regulates and aims to promote professionalism in among Construction and Project Managers in Kenya. While bringing together professionals in the industry ACMK streamlines and sets a code of conduct, and procedures of practice and ensures strict pre-qualifications for its members.

Kenya Association of Building and Civil Engineering Consultants (KABCEC)

Established in 1949 KABCEC brings together building and civil engineering contractors in Kenya. While aiming to advance the interests of contractors KABCEC takes part in policy formulation, revision, amendment and improvement of the Conditions of Contract for Building Works. The organization actively advocates for contractors ensuring fair conditions and promoting professionalism.

Kenya Federation of Master Builders (KFMB)

KFMB is a contractor’s association that develops contractors through advocacy, vocational training, networking and capacity building. Primarily KFMB strives to create an environment where contractors work efficiently, with less stress and achieve better results. They also, through cooperation and industry collaboration protect contractor’s health.

Road and Civil Engineering Contractors Association (RACECA)

RACECA aims to develop local construction capacity by promoting and encouraging training for individuals engaged in roads and civil engineering construction. It emphasizes the use of modern technology and engages in research and development. The association also encourages the adoption of efficient standards conducive to ordered development. RACECA promotes knowledge sharing and networking through summits, industry events and conferences.

Kenya Property Developers Association (KPDA)

Established in 2006, KPDA represents the residential, commercial and industrial property development sector in Kenya. Through collaboration with the government and the public, KPDA ensures rapid but organized, efficient, economical and ethical growth in the property development sector.
